Project Details

Introduction

This report presents the analysis and findings from the survey conducted among data professionals across various countries. The analysis leverages the dataset "DataProfessional" provided by Alex Freberg, focusing on demographics, career transitions, salary distributions, and job satisfaction metrics.

GitHub Link

Problem Statements

The key questions addressed in the analysis include:

  • Comparison of Salary: Male vs. Female data professionals.
  • Sample Space: Total number of survey participants.
  • Average Salary: By job categories in various countries.
  • Programmer Distribution: Number of programmers globally.
  • Highest Salary: Top-earning data professional category.
  • Career Switching Trends: Into the data industry by country.
  • Job Satisfaction:Work-life balance & Compensation average ratings.
  • Average Age: Of survey participants.

Dashboards and Visualizations

The analysis was visualized using Power BI dashboards, covering the following key metrics:

  • Salary comparisons by gender and countries.
  • Top data professions by average salary.
  • Distribution of programming language usage.
  • Career transition trends across countries.
  • Satisfaction ratings for work-life balance and compensation.

Fig 1. Data Professional Survey Analysis Dashboard

Key Insights & Findings

  • Gender Salary Comparison: Women professionals draw 2% higher salaries on average compared to men, highlighting progress in gender parity in the data industry.
  • Sample Space: The survey had 630 participants globally, providing diverse insights.
  • Average Age of Participants:The average age of survey participants was 29.87 years, indicating a young and dynamic workforce.
  • Programming Language Usage:Python is the most widely used programming language by data professionals, with data analysts utilizing it the most.
  • Salary Trends by Country: USA offers the highest average salary (~$78,000) for data professionals.
  • Top Earning Professionals:Data Scientists emerged as the highest-earning profession, with an average salary exceeding $80,000.
  • Career Switch Trends: The USA leads with the highest number of career switchers into the data industry (250+ professionals).
  • Job Satisfaction:Average rating for job satisfaction in terms of work-life balance is 5.74 and in terms of compensation is 4.27.
  • Country Leadership in Data Professionals: USA leads globally in hosting the largest number of data professionals.

Outcome

The analysis revealed key trends shaping the data professional landscape:

  • A promising shift toward gender equality in salaries.
  • Increasing adoption of Python as the go-to language for data professionals.
  • USA leads in terms of salaries, career transitions, and overall number of data professionals.
  • Areas like work-life balance and compensation satisfaction need improvement.
